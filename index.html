Welcome to Ormiston Primary Behavior Monitor

Select a Behavior

Aggressive / Bullying

  • Teach conflict resolution skills.
  • Encourage empathy and perspective-taking.
  • Set clear consequences for aggressive behavior.

Anxiety

  • Provide a safe space for calming down.
  • Teach relaxation techniques.
  • Gradually expose the student to anxiety-provoking situations with support.

Confrontational / Defensive

  • Use a calm and non-threatening approach.
  • Set clear, consistent expectations for behavior.
  • Provide positive reinforcement for appropriate responses.

Defiant

  • Give choices to encourage cooperation.
  • Maintain a calm and assertive demeanor.
  • Clearly define and consistently enforce rules.

Disorganized

  • Use visual schedules and reminders.
  • Break tasks into smaller, manageable steps.
  • Provide organizational tools and supports.

Disrespectful

  • Model respectful behavior and language.
  • Teach and practice appropriate ways to communicate.
  • Set clear expectations and consequences for disrespect.

Disruptive

  • Implement a behavior management plan.
  • Provide structured and engaging activities.
  • Use a calm and consistent response to disruptions.

Failing To Turn In Work

  • Provide regular reminders and check-ins.
  • Offer support with organization and time management.
  • Communicate with parents about homework expectations.

Frustration

  • Teach coping strategies and problem-solving skills.
  • Provide a break area for calming down.
  • Encourage communication about feelings and frustrations.

Hyperactive

  • Incorporate physical activity breaks.
  • Provide a structured and predictable environment.
  • Use visual and auditory cues to help focus.

Impulsive

  • Teach self-control strategies and techniques.
  • Provide clear and immediate feedback on behavior.
  • Use visual prompts to remind the student to think before acting.

Inappropriate Language

  • Set clear rules regarding acceptable language.
  • Teach alternative ways to express feelings and frustrations.
  • Provide immediate feedback on inappropriate language use.

Lack of Participation

  • Use engaging and interactive teaching methods.
  • Provide choices to increase student investment.
  • Offer positive reinforcement for participation.

Lack of Responsibility

  • Set clear expectations and follow through with consequences.
  • Encourage self-management through goal setting.
  • Provide opportunities for student-led responsibilities.

Lack of Social Skills

  • Teach and model appropriate social interactions.
  • Use role-playing to practice social skills.
  • Provide feedback and praise for appropriate social behavior.

Low/No Work Completion

  • Break tasks into smaller, manageable steps.
  • Provide regular check-ins and feedback.
  • Use a reward system to encourage task completion.

Lying/Cheating

  • Teach the importance of honesty and integrity.
  • Provide clear consequences for lying and cheating.
  • Encourage open communication and problem-solving.

Name Calling

  • Address the behavior immediately with clear consequences.
  • Teach respect and appropriate communication skills.
  • Encourage empathy and understanding of others' feelings.

Negative Attitude

  • Encourage a growth mindset and positive self-talk.
  • Provide support and understanding for underlying issues.
  • Model and reinforce positive behavior and attitudes.

Off-Task Disruptive

  • Use visual and auditory cues to redirect focus.
  • Provide clear and structured tasks.
  • Offer frequent breaks and incentives for on-task behavior.

Off-Task Non-Disruptive

  • Set clear expectations for staying on task.
  • Provide engaging and relevant activities.
  • Monitor and provide gentle reminders to stay focused.

Out of Seat

  • Set clear expectations for staying seated.
  • Provide movement breaks as needed.
  • Use a visual reminder or cue for staying in seat.

Poor Coping Skills

  • Teach and practice coping strategies.
  • Provide support during stressful situations.
  • Encourage the use of coping tools and techniques.

Poor Peer Relationships

  • Encourage social skills training and practice.
  • Provide opportunities for positive peer interactions.
  • Support conflict resolution and communication skills.

Poor Self Esteem

  • Encourage and reinforce strengths and achievements.
  • Provide positive feedback and support.
  • Teach self-compassion and self-acceptance.

Rushing Through Work

  • Encourage a focus on quality over speed.
  • Provide reminders to check work carefully.
  • Set specific goals and checkpoints for completing work.

Sadness / Depression

  • Provide emotional support and a listening ear.
  • Encourage participation in enjoyable activities.
  • Seek help from school counselors or mental health professionals.

Somatic Complaints

  • Assess for possible underlying issues or stressors.
  • Provide a supportive and understanding environment.
  • Encourage communication about physical symptoms and feelings.

Stealing

  • Discuss the impact of stealing and promote honesty.
  • Implement consequences for stealing.
  • Provide opportunities for making amends and restitution.

Tantrums / Out of Control

  • Use calming techniques and strategies during tantrums.
  • Provide a safe space for the student to calm down.
  • Teach emotional regulation and coping skills.

Tardiness

  • Set clear expectations for punctuality.
  • Provide reminders and support for time management.
  • Address any underlying issues causing tardiness.

Unable to Work Independently

  • Provide step-by-step guidance and support.
  • Gradually increase independence with scaffolded tasks.
  • Use visual and verbal prompts to guide work.

Unfocused / Inattentive

  • Use structured and engaging activities.
  • Provide visual and auditory cues to focus attention.
  • Incorporate short, frequent breaks to maintain attention.

Unmotivated

  • Set clear and achievable goals.
  • Provide positive reinforcement and incentives.
  • Make tasks relevant and interesting to the student.

Upset / Crying

  • Provide emotional support and a safe space.
  • Encourage communication about feelings.
  • Address any underlying issues causing distress.
